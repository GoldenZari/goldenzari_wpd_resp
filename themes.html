Themes in Epic the Musical

Main Characters

Ruthlessness

One of the most prevalent themes in Epic the Musical is "Ruthlessness." Many characters justify their actions through ruthlessness, believing it is necessary to protect themselves and their own. For example, Polyphemus in the Cyclops Saga lashes out at Odysseus' crew after they harm his sheep, believing that if none survive, the rest of his flock will be safe. Similarly, Poseidon enacts justice by punishing Odysseus and his men for harming his son.

Identity and Change

Odysseus undergoes significant transformation throughout his journey, grappling with his own identity as a leader, warrior, and father. The struggles he faces force him to reevaluate his beliefs, making him question who he is by the end of the story.

Justice and Revenge

Justice is a driving force in many sagas, often intertwined with revenge. Poseidon punishes Odysseus for harming his son, and later, Odysseus enacts revenge upon those who wronged him in Ithaca. The cycle of justice and revenge plays a crucial role in the story's moral conflicts.
